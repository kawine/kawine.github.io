As AI becomes more performant, there has been growing interest in using AI as both a complement and substitute for human labor. However, human-AI collaboration and substitution do not occur in a vacuum; they are driven by broader economic forces that are often left unexamined. My work studies the interplay of AI and these economic forces, and seeks to make AI systems that are more compatible with all economic actors, including individuals, firms, and the state.
